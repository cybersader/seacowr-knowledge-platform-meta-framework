# Output (O)

**Question:** Where and how does **finished** information EXIT to a consumer?

> SEACOW(r) is **not prescribing** "you must have an `/Output/` folder." It's asking *where does information leave your control to reach an audience?*

Outputs are the audience-facing surface. They're the **why** of the whole
system — capture and work exist to feed Output. Think of Output as the
**library** or **storefront** of your knowledge platform.

## Examples

| System | Output surfaces |
|---|---|
| Personal Obsidian vault | `Blog/`, `Published/`, `Presentations/`, `Portfolio/`, Resume.md |
| Corporate file share | `Shared/Reports/`, `Public/`, `Client Deliverables/` |
| Code repo | `dist/`, `build/`, `docs/api/`, releases, README, GitHub Pages |
| Email | Sent folder, templates |
| Physical | Outbox, archived completed work |

## Analysis: what to ask

- Where do **finished artifacts** go?
- What's **ready for an audience**?
- How do you distinguish **drafts from published work**?
- What **leaves your control** (shared, published, delivered)?
- What's the **audience contract** for each Output surface?

## Output conventions

- **Output sub-trees can nest deeply** — unlike Capture, Output benefits from
  hierarchical structure that helps navigation. Johnny Decimal–style numbering
  works well here (`01-Projects/`, `02-Tools/`, `03-Frameworks/`).
- **Output is strictly Output** — once content is in an Output sub-tree, don't
  mix raw Captures into it. The audience contract requires consistency.
- **Different audiences = different Outputs** — a recruiter's-eye-view resume
  and a deep-technical resume are two Outputs, even if the underlying Work file
  is shared.
- **Identify the Entity** for each Output — every Output exists for some
  consumer Entity (you, a team, the public, an AI agent).

## Output × Capture overlap

Captures that are also viewable as Output (e.g. a "see what I'm clipping"
public page) need to honor the stricter Output contract — clear provenance,
stable URLs, non-destructive editing.

## Output as the "library"

A useful framing: Output is the *library interface* of your knowledge
platform. Library science vocabulary applies — controlled vocabularies,
authority files, taxonomies. The more public-facing the Output, the more
library-like its conventions should be. (See
[../../05-deep-dives/knowledge-organization.md](../../05-deep-dives/knowledge-organization.md).)

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Burying Output under Capture or Work folders — readers can't find it.
- ❌ Treating Output as "wherever publishable things end up" without naming the
  contract — what makes something publishable here?
- ❌ Failing to distinguish Outputs by Entity — one Output for "everybody"
  pleases nobody.
