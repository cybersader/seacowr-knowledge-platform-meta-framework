# Capture (C)

**Question:** Where and how does information ENTER the system?

> SEACOW(r) is **not prescribing** "you must have a `/Capture/` folder." It's asking *where do raw inputs land in YOUR system?*

Captures are the surface where unprocessed information first lives. They're
deliberately low-friction — you want it easy to land things, even at the cost
of being unsorted.

## Examples

| System | Capture surfaces |
|---|---|
| Personal Obsidian vault | `Inbox/`, `Clippings/`, `Quick Notes/`, daily-notes |
| Corporate file share | `Teams/<name>/Drop/`, `Incoming/`, `Submissions/` |
| Code repo | `docs/ADRs/`, issue templates, `raw-data/`, scratch branches |
| Email | Inbox, unread messages |
| Physical desk | In-tray, sticky notes, the pile of mail |

## Analysis: what to ask

- Where do **raw inputs** land?
- How do you **save things for later** without committing to a structure?
- What's your **inbox** (whatever you call it)?
- Where does **unprocessed information** live?
- How does Capture **distinguish from Work** (so things don't get stuck unprocessed)?

## Capture rules (common conventions)

- **Keep capture nesting shallow** — typical limit is 1–2 levels. Deep
  hierarchies inside Capture suggest you're really doing Work in disguise.
- **Capture is unsorted by design** — don't pre-categorize obsessively.
  Categorization is Work; doing it at Capture time creates friction that kills
  the capture habit.
- **Capture sub-areas** can exist by *source* (Clippings/YouTube/, Clippings/Articles/)
  or by *audience-of-eventual-output* (Capture/Public/, Capture/Team/). Avoid
  by-topic — that's a Work concern.

## Capture × Output overlap

Some captures can simultaneously serve as Output ("see what I'm clipping" pages
that the public can browse). When this happens, name the dual-use explicitly
and keep the Capture-side conventions stricter (clearer source provenance,
non-destructive editing) to protect the Output contract.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Forcing a folder named "Capture" because the framework names one. Call it
  whatever fits your workflow (`Inbox/`, `Drops/`, `Triage/`).
- ❌ Building deep hierarchies inside the inbox — defeats the purpose.
- ❌ Mixing finished work into Capture areas — once it's Output, move it.
